Travel to Montenegro

Introduction

Montenegro is a beautiful country sitting on the southeastern part of Europe. Its capital, Podgorica, is the largest city and has a population of over 600,000 people.

Places to Visit

Montenegro has several stunning places to visit. Let's explore some of the top places you must visit:

  • Podgorica
  • Niagara Waterfalls
  • Kotor
  • Budva
  • Our Lady of the Rocks

Podgorica

  1. Stara Varoš: This is an old Ottoman quarter having a quiet, lived‑in charm.
  2. Millennium Bridge — This is a modern landmark stretching over the Morača River and suitable for spectacular sunset views.
  3. Ribnica Bridge — This is a 16th‑century stone bridge where the Ribnica river meets the Morača river.
  4. Gorica Hill Park — This is a pine‑covered hill with stunning walking trails and the city’s best viewpoint.
  5. King’s Park — This peaceful green park can be found in the centre of the city next to the Millennium Bridge.
  6. Doclea (Duklja) Ruins — This is a quiet, atmospheric town showing the remains of a Roman city just outside Podgorica.
  7. Plantaze Šipčanik Wine Cellar — This is a massive underground wine cellar inside an old military bunker ideal for wine tastings.
  8. Morača River Canyon — This is a dramatic stretch of turquoise water cutting right through the city.

Niagara Waterfalls

Niagara Waterfalls in Montenegro is one of those places that leaves you breathless because you don’t expect such a powerful, wide, turquoise‑green cascade to appear just minutes from Podgorica.

Kotor

Kotor wraps you in its dramatic beauty, with towering limestone cliffsdropping straight into a glass‑calm bay that feels almost unreal.

Budva

Mogren Beach is arguably the best known and most attractive of the Budva city beaches, nestled beneath the cliffs of the Spas hill.

Our Lady of the Rocks

This is one of the two islets off the coast of Perast in the Bay of Kotor, Montenegro. The church is built on a completely man‑made island a few hundred metres off the quayside in Perast.
